Getting everything together to cook.

At least once a month my mother would make "Arroz con Pollo". It was one of my favorite dishes. My mom would go to "el vivero" a place where they sold live poultry. Get a hen and then head over to "el mercado" the market place. Where she would buy the rice and all the herbs use to make "el sorifto". You cannot make spanish food without sorifto we use in everything. It amazing that someone has not make a perfume out of it. You just get this happy feeling when you start making it. The smell takes over the house and everyone know something wonderful is being made.

Recipe for Sorifto

  1. 2 bunches of culantro
  2. 2 bunches of cilantro
  3. 5 spanish white onions
  4. 5 cuban peppers
  5. 2 whole head of garlic
  6. 8 sweet peppers
  7. 6 ripe tomatoes
Clean cilantro and culantro in lots of water to get rid all sand and dirt. Next peeled all the onions and garlic. Chop and de-vein the peppers. Cut up the tomatoes in pieces. Then blend all the ingredients in a blender. Pour into clean jar and use for all your dishes.

Recipe for Arroz con Pollo

  • 4 cups medium grain white rice
  • 4 lbs. chicken (any parts you like)
  • 1/4 cup chopped red peppers
  • 1/4 cup chopped green peppers
  • 1/2 cup chopped spanish white onion
  • 3 tablespoons stuffed olives
  • 2 teaspoons capers
  • 1 can of red roasted peppers
  • 1/2 cup sorifto
  • 1 chicken bouillon
  • 1 packet sazon with culantro y achiote
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 6 cups water
  • salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 12 ounces can of tomato sauce
  • Dutch oven with lid

Preparing

  1. Step 1 In a Dutch oven or large cast-iron skillet, heat the oil over moderately high heat. Season the chicken with the salt and pepper, and cook until all sides are brown, approximately 5 minutes. Remove the chicken and set aside, reducing the heat on the skillet to moderate.
  2. Step 2 Add the onion and sweat for three minutes, until soft and translucent. Add the olives, capers, tomato sauce and sofrito and cook for five minutes. Add the rice, browned chicken and water and bring to a gentle boil over moderately high heat. Cook, uncovered, until the water is mostly absorbed, about 25 minutes. Gently stir the rice, cover and cook over low heat for another 20 minutes. Remove from the heat and serve
